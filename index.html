About Us

A Legacy of Classical Dance & Artistry

Ghungroo Kathak Academy is a premier dance institution dedicated to preserving and promoting the timeless art of Kathak. Founded by a certified Kathak artist and performer with over 30 years of teaching experience, our academy blends the richness of classical tradition with the vibrancy of contemporary Bollywood and fusion styles.

We welcome students of all ages and skill levels — from beginners taking their first steps to advanced dancers preparing for stage performances. Our structured curriculum, warm environment, and passionate instructors ensure every student flourishes.

Ghungroo Kathak Academy is affiliated to Pracheen Kala Kendra. Our students appear in exams every year. We conduct certified courses and choreograph Bollywood Kathak too. We train students online across the world.

Year-Wise Syllabus & Study Materials

Click on any year below to expand the full syllabus. Each section includes a downloadable Practice Question Paper to help students prepare for their Pracheen Kala Kendra examinations.

🏃 Practical (75 Marks)
  • Physical exercises: Head Movements, Shoulder Movements
  • To walk in Rhythm and to clap with music
  • Two varieties of Tatkars in Teentaal (Thah, Dugun, Chaugun) with hand posture
  • Two easy Tukda in Teentaal
  • One thematic dance in Keharwa Taal
  • Padhant (recitation) of Tukda and Tatkar prescribed in the syllabus
  • Prescribed Taals: Teentaal and Keharwa
📚 Theory (25 Marks)
  • Knowledge of: Tali, Khali and Avartan
  • Knowledge of Laya — Thah (single), Dugun (double) and Chaugun (quadruple)
  • Knowledge of Tatkar
  • Definition of: Nritta, Nritya, Natya
📄 Key Definitions
  • Tali: A clap to mark the stressed beats of a Taal
  • Khali: An empty wave of the hand for unstressed beats
  • Avartan: One complete cycle of a Taal from Sam to Sam
  • Tatkar: Basic rhythmic footwork — the foundation of Kathak
  • Teentaal: 16-beat rhythmic cycle divided as 4+4+4+4
  • Keharwa: 8-beat rhythmic cycle (4+4)
  • Nritta: Pure dance — rhythmic movement without expression
  • Nritya: Expressive dance combining rhythm and emotion
  • Natya: Dramatic dance — storytelling through movement

🏃 Practical (75 Marks)
  • Griwa Sanchalan (Neck Movements)
  • Demonstration of two different types of Tatkars in Teentaal
  • Performance of five simple Tukda in Teentaal
  • Aamad in Teentaal
  • A dance in Keharwa Taal

📚 Theory (25 Marks)
  • Knowledge of Sam, Khali, Taal and Laya (Thah, Dugun and Chaugun)
  • Definition of Lasya and Tandava
  • Practice recitation bol of Salami and Tatkar
📄 Key Definitions
  • Sam: The first and most important beat of any Taal
  • Lasya: Graceful, feminine aspect of dance associated with Goddess Parvati
  • Tandava: Vigorous, masculine aspect of dance associated with Lord Shiva
  • Aamad: Persian word for 'entrance'; a composition danced at the beginning of a recital
  • Salami: A salutation composition performed at the start, paying respect to the audience
  • Griwa Sanchalan: Neck movements — one of the key physical exercises in Kathak training

🏃 Practical (100 Marks)
  • Practicing neck and shoulder physical exercises
  • Practice of walking in Lay and Chhand
  • Two types of Tatkar in Teentaal in Thah, Dugun and Chaugun Lay with hand posture
  • Eight simple Toda and Tukda
  • Four simple Gat Nikas
  • Ability to dance on Baddh Geet in Dadra and Keharwa Taal
  • Practice of recitation by showing all the Tukda and Tatkar on hand prescribed in the syllabus
  • Note: The course of full years will be combined
📚 Theory (50 Marks)
  • Definition of: Lay, Taal, Matra, Sam, Tali, Khali, Aavartan, Vibhag, Theka, Nrit, Tatkar, Gat Palta, Amad, Paran, Nritya, Natay, Tandav and Salami
  • Practice of writing Theka in Thah and Dugun Lay of Taals prescribed in the syllabus
  • Write immediately in Ekgun (Thah), Dugun and Chaugun
  • Biography of: Shri Shambhu Maharaj and Achhan Maharaj
📄 Key Definitions
  • Vibhag: A section or division within a Taal cycle
  • Theka: The standard set of bols (syllables) that define a particular Taal
  • Paran: A rhythmic composition ending with a Tihai, usually in pure rhythm
  • Gat Nikas: A mime composition showing a character entering the stage
  • Shambhu Maharaj: Legendary Kathak Guru of the Lucknow Gharana, known for Abhinaya

🏃 Practical (100 Marks)
  • Aandolan of shoulder, head, waist etc. in Lay and Taal
  • Thaat, Kasak, Masak and Kataksh
  • Practice of performing in Vilambit Lay those from the previous year's syllabus
  • In Teentaal: Twelve Toda, Two Paran, Two Aamad, One Salami, Two Kavit
  • In Jhaptaal: Thah and Dugan Mentkar, Two Paran, Six Toda
  • Aadhunik Nritya in Teentaal, Dadra and Keharwa Taal
  • Demonstration of five Bhav including special facial expressions
  • Four types of Griwa Sanchalan and eight types of Head Sanchalan
  • Practical knowledge of one hand posture (Asamyukta Hasta)
  • Dance practice in Teentaal, Jhaptaal, Keharwa and Dadra Taal
  • Ability to perform the Theka of appropriate Taals through Thah, Dugun and Chaugun
  • Note: Syllabus of previous years will be combined
📚 Theory (50 Marks)
  • Knowledge regarding the purpose and importance of Ghungroo in dance and what type of Ghungroo should be used in Kathak dance
  • (a) Six Ang   (b) Six Pratyang and their Upaang   (c) Eight Nayan Bhav   (d) Four types of Griwa Sanchalan   (e) Six Bhrkuti Sanchalan
  • Introduction to life of: Guru Narayan Prasad ji and Vinda Deen Maharaj ji
  • Definition of: Kavitt, Chakrad Paran, Gat, Asamyukta Mudra
  • Practice of writing Tukda, Paran etc. in Taal Lipi in Taals prescribed in the syllabus
  • Knowledge of Bhatkhande Taal Lipi Paddhati
📄 Key Definitions
  • Kavitt: A poetic composition set to a rhythmic cycle, often recited and danced simultaneously
  • Chakardar Paran: A Paran that is repeated three times, each ending on Sam
  • Six Ang: Head, Hands, Chest, Sides, Waist and Feet — the six main body parts in dance
  • Bhatkhande Taal Lipi: The notation system developed by Pt. V.N. Bhatkhande for writing rhythmic compositions

🏃 Practical (100 Marks)
  • Aandolan of Kati (waist), Uru (thighs), Bhrkuti (eyebrows) in Lay and Taal
  • In Teentaal: Four types of Tatkar and their Paltas, Two Purana Aamad, Two Salami, Two Chakardar Paran, Three Kavit, Four Gat Bhav and Nikas
  • In Jhaptaal: Four types of Tatkar and their Paltas, Two Aamad, One Salami, Two Chakardar Toda, Two Chakardar Paran
  • In Ektaal: Two types of Tatkar and their Paltas, Four Toda, One Aamad, One Salami, Two Paran, One Chakardar Paran
  • In Dhamar: Two types of Tatkar and their Paltas, Two Toda, One Aamad, One Salami, Two Paran, One Chakardar Paran
  • Gat Nikas, Ghunghat, Matki etc. with Bhav
  • Thaat, Kasak, Masak etc.
  • Padhant in all Taals of the syllabus and previous years
  • Ability to differentiate Lucknow and Jaipur Gharana by demonstrating Toda, Paran and Gat
  • Practice of Teentaal, Jhaptaal, Dhamar and Ektaal in Thah, Dugun, Tigun and Chaugun Lay
  • Note: Syllabus of previous years will be combined
📚 Theory (50 Marks)
  • Definition of: Kasak, Masak, Kataksh, Thaat, Guru, Laghu, Drut, Anudrut
  • Essay on: Kathak and Manipuri dance
  • Difference between Kathak and Natwari dance
  • Brief history of Indian dance
  • Definition of Ras and Bhav
  • Dress and costume in Kathak dance
  • Meaning of Abhinaya and its explanation
  • Knowledge of Bhatkhande and Vishnu Digambar Taal Lipi Paddhati
  • Introduction to life of: Pt. Birju Maharaj, Lachhu Maharaj, Kartik Ram
📄 Key Definitions
  • Kasak-Masak: Subtle movements of the wrists and torso performed during Thaat posture
  • Abhinaya: The art of expression in dance — conveying emotions through facial expressions and gestures
  • Ras: The aesthetic emotional essence experienced by the audience during a performance
  • Pt. Birju Maharaj: The greatest exponent of the Lucknow Gharana of Kathak in the 20th century

🏃 Practical (100 Marks)
  • In Teentaal: Tough Tatkar and their various types, Two Chakkaradar Paran, Paran of Tistra Jati, Two Pramilu Toda, Three Kavit, Demonstration through Holi, Makhan Chori, Kaliya Daman, Ghooghant, Nikas or Gat Bhav
  • In Ektaal: Tatkar and its types, Two Aamad, Two Paran, Two Chakkaradar Paran, Six Toda
  • In Ada Char Taal: Tatkar in different Laykari, An Aamad, A Salami, Two Paran, Six Toda
  • Special practice of dance in Teentaal, Ektaal, Jhaptaal and Dhamar Taal
  • Practice of playing Teentaal, Ektaal, Jhaptaal and Dhamar Taal on Thah, Dugun, Tigun and Chaugun Laykari on Tabla
  • Practice of two Toda in each including Tatkar in Sool Taal, Rupak and Sawari Taal
  • Demonstration of Aadi Kuwadi Lay in dance
  • Practice of playing Nagma on Harmonium in Teentaal, Ektaal, Jhaptaal
  • Note: Syllabus of previous years will be combined
📚 Theory (50 Marks)
  • Introduction to various Gharanas of Kathak dance and their comparative study
  • Knowledge about Indian dances: Kathak, Manipuri, Kathakali, Bharat Natyam, Odissi, Kuchipudi etc.
  • Study of Lok dances of North India and their characteristics
  • Importance of Ras in dance
  • Detailed study of Acting and its place in dance
  • Knowledge of different types of Nayak and Nayika
  • Origin of Taal and its utility in dance
  • Biography of: Shri Ram Gopal (Kathak), Pt. Charanarishra Chand, Tirath Ram Azad, Mrs. Shobna Narayan
  • Definition of: Kalash, Bhramari, Pakshi Paran, Pramilu, Nikas, Ek Pad Bhramari, Chalanchari, Bhav, Anubhav, Stuti, Mudra, Geet, Jati, Paran, Vale etc.
  • Knowledge of make up and costumes in Kathak dance
  • Knowledge of Granth: Abhinaya Darpan, Natyashastra, Darshanrup of Dhananjay
📄 Key Definitions
  • Bhramari: A spinning movement in Kathak — a pirouette or turn
  • Pramilu Toda: A composition with a specific rhythmic pattern used in Visharad level
  • Ada Char Taal: A 14-beat rhythmic cycle used in advanced Kathak
  • Abhinaya Darpan: A classical treatise on dance and gesture by Nandikesvara

🏃 Practical (100 Marks)
  • Mastery over all Taals: Teentaal, Jhaptaal, Ektaal, Dhamar, Roopak, Chautaal
  • Advanced Tatkar patterns with complex Layakaris (Aad, Kuad, Biyad)
  • Performance of traditional Vandana or Stuti
  • Complex Chakardar Parans and Parmelus
  • Detailed Gat Bhav sequences (e.g., Makhan Chori, Kaliya Daman)
  • Ability to improvise (Upaj) in Thaat and Tatkar
  • Full solo recital capability: Aamad, Salami, Tatkar, Gat, Thumri, Tarana
  • Padhant of all compositions with precise Tali and Khali
📚 Theory (50 Marks)
  • Comparative study of Kathak with other classical dance forms (Bharatanatyam, Odissi, etc.)
  • Detailed study of the Natyashastra and Abhinaya Darpan
  • The role of music (Vocal and Instrumental) in Kathak dance
  • Study of Nayak-Nayika Bhed (Classification of Heroes and Heroines)
  • The evolution of Kathak costumes and stage presentation
  • Writing notation of a full Kathak recital (Vandana to Tarana)
📄 Key Definitions
  • Natyashastra: Ancient Indian treatise on performing arts by Bharata Muni
  • Abhinaya Darpan: Classical text detailing gestures and movements in Indian dance
  • Tarana: A musical composition using syllables like 'Ta Na Dir Tom'
  • Jatis: Rhythmic subdivisions — Tisra(3), Chatushra(4), Khanda(5), Misra(7), Sankeerna(9)

“Archana Sinha is one of the most passionate and dedicated teachers I have come across. She is a perfectionist who can make a non-dancer a great dancer — because more than the dancer, she believes in herself as a soulful teacher. She makes true the ABCD of dance: Anybody Can Dance.” — Tabassum Ali, Student, Ghungroo Kathak Academy

Archana Sinha was born in Kolkata and is an accomplished academician and performing artist. She holds a B.Sc. from Kolkata University, a Post-Graduate Diploma in Public Relations from Rajendra Prasad Institute, Mumbai, and a B.Ed. from Annamalai University. A true all-rounder, she has won numerous awards in inter-school and college competitions in dance, debate, recitation, extempore, mime, and sports. She is also a certified NCC cadet and Civil Defence certificate holder from West Bengal.

Archana began her Kathak training at the age of 10 and completed her diploma from Prayag Sangeet Samiti. She further refined her classical skills under the guidance of Padmashree Mrs. Alaknanda Bose in Noida and received further advanced training under the tutelage of Shri Arjun Mishra ji. She also holds an additional diploma in Kuchipudi under the tutelage of Shri Prabeer Dutta.

In 2016, Archana moved to Kuwait and joined DPS Kuwait, where she was swiftly promoted to Head of Department – Dance in 2017. Determined to promote the classical art of North Indian Kathak, she founded Ghungroo Kathak Academy in Kuwait, running two centres in Salmiya Block 10 and Mangaf, teaching over 50 students aged 7 to 50 years.

Ghungroo quickly gained recognition through landmark performances — including the concert of Kailash Kher (2017), the Bollywood Tollywood Dhamaka with Chief Guest Piyush Bhagat, and Dhoom Macha Le, where students performed before Dance India Dance celebrities Punit Pathak and Mudassar Khan. At the prestigious VIBE 2018 event organised by D-Illusions, Ghungroo students’ Kathak–Hip Hop fusion earned thunderous applause and personal praise from Bollywood choreographer Dharmesh.

Archana has choreographed a wide spectrum of dance dramas and productions, including Kannagi, Cinemania, Navras, River Rhapsody, Noor Jahaan, Swan Lake, Sound of Music, Udaan (Women Empowerment), Taking Root (Environment), and Service Before Self.

Today, Ghungroo Kathak Academy continues to grow under her leadership in Surrey, BC, Canada, offering in-person and online classes to students across the world. Her students have won numerous awards across India, Dubai, Qatar, Kuwait, and Canada, and continue to carry forward the legacy of classical Kathak with pride.
